How the library works

No library card, no waitlist, no late fees. The whole point is friction-free access to the textbooks you need for the semester.

Search the catalog

Filter by subject, title, author, or ISBN. If we have it, you'll see it listed as available.

Request a book

Tap request, tell us who you are and how to reach you. A volunteer will email you within 48 hours.

Pick it up

Drop by the library during open hours. Take the book, use it for as long as you need, return it when you're done — or don't. It's yours.

About the library

The Free Textbook Library started as a simple idea — students shouldn't have to choose between eating and buying a $300 biology textbook. We take donated books from graduating students, faculty, and community members, log them, and put them back into circulation.

Everything is volunteer-run. Every book is free. We don't track who has what or when it comes back. The only rule: when you're done, pass it on — either back to us or to another student who needs it.

If you have books to donate or want to volunteer with inventory, maintenance, or outreach, reach out through the contact links below.
